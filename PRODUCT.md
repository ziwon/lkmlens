# Product

## Register

product

## Users

LKMLens serves BSP, platform, firmware, systems, robotics, automotive, and
product engineers who need to understand upstream Linux changes without
following every kernel mailing list. Kernel maintainers and contributors can
use the same evidence, but the primary workflow is product planning: identify
changes that touch owned hardware, understand their review and integration
state, and decide when investigation, testing, or backport work should begin.

## Product Purpose

LKMLens is an open-source kernel ecosystem observatory. It turns public patch,
review, maintainer-tree, mainline, release, stable, and Android common-kernel
evidence into curated product signals by topic and hardware vendor.

Its promise is:

> Understand what kernel changes mean for your hardware and product.

Success means a non-kernel-specialist can distinguish an interesting proposal
from a change that is reviewed, accepted by a maintainer, released upstream,
or available to a downstream product kernel. LKMLens never predicts merge
likelihood; it reports observed evidence and makes missing coverage explicit.

## Brand Personality

Precise, composed, and forward-looking. The voice is technically credible
without assuming maintainer-level knowledge. It should feel like a healthy
open-source engineering community: transparent about evidence, uncertainties,
coverage gaps, and contribution paths.

## Anti-references

- A generic mailing-list search archive with summaries attached.
- A generic AI dashboard that hides sources behind confident prose.
- A stock-trading signal product that turns patches into speculative scores.
- A terminal cosplay interface that requires kernel-community jargon to use.
- A marketing landing page made from interchangeable icon-card grids.

## Design Principles

1. **Lead with product consequence.** Show affected hardware, integration
   stage, stakeholders, and the next useful action before raw mail volume.
2. **Observed, not predicted.** Every lifecycle state is backed by a public
   source; unknown and not-yet-observed are first-class states.
3. **Progressive technical depth.** A product engineer gets an immediate
   orientation while a kernel developer can still inspect trailers, revisions,
   messages, commits, and canonical sources.
4. **Curation is a public artifact.** Topic and vendor lenses are explainable,
   linkable, and open to corrections rather than personalized black boxes.
5. **Calm density.** Support serious scanning and comparison without visual
   noise, decorative dashboards, or oversized marketing composition.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Meaning must never depend on color alone. Keyboard access,
visible focus, semantic landmarks, readable technical typography, responsive
layouts, and reduced-motion behavior are required. Public product copy is
English-first for broad open-source participation and should avoid unexplained
maintainer jargon wherever a plain-language label is possible.
